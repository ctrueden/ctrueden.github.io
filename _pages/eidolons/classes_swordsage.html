Swordsage

 

      Legend speaks of a time in the earliest dawn of the world, before the rise of the humanoid peoples. According to these tales, conflict and warfare were concepts unfamiliar to the primordial beings that walked the planes. As the story goes, forces of good and evil were also absent, though with the coming of mankind, such a void could not remain. As the first act of benevolence and cooperation took place, so too was born the first act of malevolence and agression. With contact came conflict, and soon the forces of this conflict grew beyond the actions of mortals which perpetuated them; they became beings unto themselves. 

      These new beings sought to grind the peoples of the world, as well the living spirit of the world itself, to an ashen waste, soaked in blood and misery. It was said that the old spirits of creation; those who built the order of the universe, and those who broke the unused back to its component chaos; took up the cause of protecting the fledgling races of man. Together, they fought back these beings of hate, cruelty, and opressive darkness. When at last they had freed the world from their enemies' advance, it is said they left a legacy to the races of man, that they might hold them in stewardship, protecting their world. These were known as the twin fists of creation. 

      The Way of the Heaven Fist was the way of order and binding, the way of knowing structure and purpose, as well as preserving equilibrium, and dicipline. The way of the Heaven fist is to be aware of the many threads and bonds that make up both one's self, and one's opponent. To strike with clarity of purpose at the weakest point of one's enemy is the Heaven Fist's strength. The virtues it espouses are temperance, patience, and grace. Its techniques often manifest affinity with light and cold. The primary weakness of the Heaven fist is a perceived emotional detachment that comes from an emphasis on objective dicipline. 

      The Way of the Hell Fist was the way of punishment and freedom, the way of calling upon the most furious of personal strengths in order to overcome a dire need. Driven both by fury at ones own opression, and a burning need to see the cruel brought low, The Hell fist drives one past the limitations of their mortal flesh, that they might aid the weak and bear the burden of destroying an otherwise superior foe.To call upon the true reserves of strength needed by this art, one must incite powerful and passionate feelings. As such, a student of the way can find its use as emotionally draining as it is physically so. Many of its techniques relate to fire, as well as seemingly inhuman applications of direct power.The virtues of the Hell fist are Valor, Compassion, and Fortitude. The weakness of the Hell fist lies in the unpredictable nature of the passion it inspires and requires.
