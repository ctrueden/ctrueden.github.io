Initial perceptions

Kumo, looking at the missing mural stones from Olidammara, has a strong feeling that one version of him stole them long ago.

Before she travels up the stairs into the main Temple area, Iraja is immediately struck with a powerful vision, a drawing on the cave wall:

  • There is a circle in the middle, with six subtrees emerging, evenly spaced.
  • The top circle is bound in chains
  • The bottom circle is burnt out, rent asunder by some force
  • From each of the six circles, an infinite fractal tree emerges

 

Arwel's shield, the Quickspur's Ally

Arwel's shield comes into play here:

  • A bashing shield for mounted combat, enchanted by the power of Hextor
  • Can be wielded by both good (Heironeous-compatible) and evil (Hextor-compatible) beings
  • Arwel notices the shield begin to resonate with the H/H shrine
  • Shield can block Vance's death attacks against Arwel or an adjacent ally

 

Vance

Talk between PCs and Vance regarding the situation with Sahrit.

  • Vance wants Dark group to convince Sahrit that his plan to invert nexuses is a recipe for disaster.
  • If Sahrit agrees to swear an Oath to Vecna (on Vecna's ruby eye) not to invert any more nexuses, or interfere at the Temple, he will be satisfied.
  • If not, Vance will Trap the Soul evil Sahrit into the gem and place it back in his eye socket. Good Sahrit remains, sans hand print.
  • If the timing is right, Vance extracting his hand print can trigger the 10pm quake.

After discussing matters between everyone, hopefully Vance convinces them to let him steal away evil Sahrit into a gem. If they are suspicious of his motives, he will even invite them back to his sanctuary to witness his experiments.

 

Appearance of the "gods"

Asmodeus uses his allies, including the other lords of hell, to masquerade as a few deities:

  1. "Kord" – actually Bel
  2. "Erathis" – actually Dispater (another Lucifer)
    • Expresses that the proper use of the Temple may be the key to perfecting civilization, and enacted worldwide peace and prosperity
    • Its misuse would destroy civilization itself
  3. "Erythnul" – actually Mammon
  4. "Corellon" and "Ehlonna" – Belial and Fierna
  5. "Moradin" – actually Levistus
    • Stresses the mercy that they have already shown to Asmodeus's people, through the inversion of the nexuses
  6. "Sehanine" – actually the Hag Countess (Lolth)
    • If the existence of the Temple were known, it would be another excuse for people to war with each other
  7. (Baalzebul—the first Emperor of Durus—is absent, since his form is cursed and cannot be changed)
  8. "Gruumsh" – actually Mephistopheles (another Lucifer)
    • While I relish the strongest of my people surviving by claiming nexuses, their destruction pleases me more
    • There will be other reasons to war, and more of my people to carry out such efforts
  9. Asmodeus – him/herself (majestic devil version)
  10. "Bahamut" – actually Tiamat

Asmodeus's goal is to persuade the party to act as "Watchers" in the coming war, pledging to remain open-minded to each other's perspectives, keep the secret of the Temple, and when the time comes, to work together invoking the true power of the Temple: eliminating the world's dependence on nexus energy.

  • The goal is to eliminate that reliance on nexus energy in order to safeguard her people.
  • It is necessary to eliminate the nexuses so that everyone can coexist on even terms.
  • The Temple must ultimately be destroyed for this to occur.
  • In the meantime, no one else must know of the Temple's existence.

Other deities chime in "for real" in support of Asmodeus's plan, to her extreme surprise:

  • Cuthbert's voice
    • Cannot appear, but speaks to Arwel's mind directly: "This is my will as well; vengeance will be served"
  • Nerull – supports the plan
    • Nerull appears, looking like death, with scythe, robes/cowl, clinking of chains—and looking at her face means death
    • Admatha senses that Nerull is bent on destroying this place, according to a plan
  • Olidammara – in the form of Shiki and Shinkumo
    • Olidammara is never deceived, because his portfolio is too trickery-centric
    • both claim to be Olidammara, and eye each other suspiciously
    • happy one walks out of the portrait
    • sad one walks in through the door (and immediately closes it)
    • Kumo can sense that they are kitsune, and thus feels an innate repulsion to them
    • They recognize Cameron: "he's one of THOSE" "oh THOSE, how curious" "yes, curious"
    • happy one supports the plan, sad one is against it
    • they argue, and convince each other, and then happy one is against, and sad is for
    • eventually, they both look at Kumo and simultaneously ask, "Kumo, what do you think?"
  • Garl Glittergold
    • He can see through Wrin, and thus learns about Asmodeus's ability to deceive at the cursed temples
    • Thus, he realizes something extremely strange is going on, but decides not to appear in person and his reveal his hand.
    • He continues to watch through Wrin, and decides to speak as Wrin: "Beware. Masterful trickery. Confusing. Dangerous. Speak more later."
    • Wrin's head lights up: green=truth, yellow=suspicious, red=known lie (as believed by Garl)
  • The Guild
    • Too dangerous for any more of them to be there, and risk being discovered by the gods
    • But since Cameron is already there, he is to learn all he can and report back through the ring
    • They will then discuss the next steps

They may be able to commune with various gods while at the temple, and obtain answers. However:

  • Asmodeus interferes with the commune and gives answers to her liking.
  • Tells them that it is imperative that no one find this place; the fate of the world depends on it.
  • She attempts to persuade them to visit a place of her choosing, where she can arrange for them to die.
