Consequences of the Ordering

  • When Tat'enda was Ordered, the quakes there magnified.
  • These quakes encompassed a number of worlds.
  • All of those worlds have become subject to strange probabilistic effects: possibility mining.
  • This allowed the Brotherhood to "probabilistically" strike at three targets simultaneously, then strike at Heimdall later despite losses.
  • Jonas is an ancient elf, currently in Tat'enda, in the eye, but he taps into the barrier using druidic magics that predate the nexuses.
    • Was a young child when the nexuses first were created.
    • Doesn't know much if anything about the war of the gods.
    • Subscribed to the Fey philosophy for a few millennia, as leader of the Harion tribe
    • Got upset when the gnomes and eladrin broke from the fey, and vowed to do something about this "cataclysm"
    • Targeting only nexuses that are probablistically inverted
    • Jonas has observed that occasionally people fall away from the path of nature, and consequently develop a rapport with the inverted nexuses; as such, he has gained a unique understanding of nexuses, giving him insight into the Ordering ritual, which is designed to return things back to the Prime state, collapsing the worlds back together
      • Three geomancers are needed to Order a nexus:
        1. Empower the nexus with spiritual energy
        2. Force a quake
        3. Collapse the nexus
    • If enough nexuses are Ordered (i.e., enough links in the chain are broken), throughout the material realm, Tharizdun is free by definition.
    • Previously was loyal to Obad-Hai's philosophy, but Obad-Hai has been absent: "Obad-Hai is dead!"
  • Wee Jas is now guiding Jonas:
    • She has told him: the Ordering will reunite the world. One past, one future. No more withered, no more withering.
    • Wee Jas wants to start again, after Tharizdun is released and creation is undone, but Jonas doesn't know that.
    • She has temples, and worshippers, but never grants spells to them (as far as anyone knows).
    • Bardic knowledge:
      • Sometimes people try to shut down the temples, but when that happens those responsible suffer horrible misfortune which always ends in death (30+: every single one of those people have died in very unlikely ways—and the flow doesn't return them).
      • A deva once wanted to end his "suffering" by terminating his rebirth cycle. He sacrificed himself on the altar in a temple of Wee Jas, and his corpse remained (usually it disappears).
      • There are warlocks who claim to have made a pact with Wee Jas for their power rather than a devil, though this is extremely rare. In actuality, the master warlock of Tane'klenya forged his pact with Wee Jas.
