Bael'turath

Baalzebul was copied. One copy entered the flow, and currently resides at Bael'turath.

  • There are rumors about a group of rogue warlocks in Bael'turath, who have seceded from the guild, led by the "Ebon Priest" (actually Baalzebul).
  • He relinquished his lordship over the seventh (the other Baalzebul has it), and is now integrated into the flow.
  • His faction wants to overthrow Asmodeus, and break his control over the warlocks.
  • If told about the nexus in balance, and convinced of its veracity, he would be willing to commit his forces.
  • The Ebon Priest has taken over the Bael'turath warlock palace, and is working with the ghost of Emperor 
  • Tudor Mabyn Gwladus fled Tat'enda, and his infernal master beckoned him to Bael'turath. He was supposed to be a spy for Master Fiendgaze but he defected for real.
  • The oni warlock Al'qadr ("fate, power") has been directly aiding Beardthicket. He taught the other defectors how to use their infernal powers to steal life energy from others, to avoid withering.

The administration:

  • Baltazar Sh'chepan – Matron's son; magistrate at Bael'turath (male because the tieflings are more patriarchal)
  • Farazar Fiendgaze – master warlock of Tane'klenya, returned to Bael'turath to put down the warlock insurgence (master warlock of Bael'turath is dead, killed by the Ebon Priest)

 

Io

Was used as a focus during the Tharizdun imprisonment ritual. Io saw all of time in a single burst of insight, driving him slightly mad.

Over time Io's closeness to Tharizdun fractured his mind in half, but he created the dragon orbs before fully succumbing to it, to help control his power. Bahamut's will imparted to Peacekeeper, to collapse all the nexuses, is in response to a vision of a world without nexuses (and because the inverted nexuses empower Tiamat). But he knew that a better way would emerge, though exactly what he was uncertain. Peacekeepr was told to be watchful of this better way, so when the party mentions the nexus in balance, Peacekeeper is eager to help.
