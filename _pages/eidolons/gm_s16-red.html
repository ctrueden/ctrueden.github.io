S-16: Red

JON: The T'Sen warrior tribe

 

Migration

  • Arwel leaves for Tat'enda.
  • Za'har & Sahrit leave for Manavendra.
  • Dariel, Kith, Simone & Sophia head to the T'Sen tribe.
  • Kith & Sophia quake out, and Admatha, Biger and Ed quake in.

Group: Admatha, Biger, Dariel, Ed, Simone

Quake happens after a few hours' travel.

 

T'Sen

Leaders:

  • Daermaethor ("great warrior") — male elven sael'dur warrior
    • gestalt ranger 15 / monk 15
    • Taurion ("huge, giant") — bonded gray render, huge, 20 HD
  • Eglerion ("praised, venerable") — male elven sael'dur elder
    • gestalt druid 15 / rogue 15
    • Bregolien ("fierce") — griffon animal companion
  • Other, younger sael'dur as well, many fine warriors, etc.

 

Culture:

  • Quite different from Idhrenion (watchers), Haerion (recluses), Baenallia (harvesters), Gearnon (pacifists)
  • Much more youthful, willing to get involved
  • Personality traits:
    • Respectful of sael'dur but less formal than other tribes
    • More tolerant and used to visitors—very friendly, except in rare cases
    • Mildly arrogant about their lifestyle—they assume that visitors will be weaker and need protection, usually
    • Even when ambushed, rather than express fear they grin broadly and exclaim their war cry
  • Rituals include:
    • gray render bonding ceremony (if it fails, the cub is killed)
    • pre-battle body painting is done but not particularly formal
    • new life being born is very serious—parents are told they must teach the child to survive effectively
    • coming of age is very serious—young people are warned they must now ultimately rely on themselves
    • death is celebrated with a festival, especially if the deaths saved other lives, or the battle went well as a result
  • Big emphasis on body paint along the arms and legs (grants divine favor effect—but not on the face; "paste gets in your eyes")
  • Major deity: Ehlonna—she is more youthful and involved than Obad-Hai
  • Also big: Corellon—often depicted as Ehlonna's father in their culture, and a mighty warrior
  • Many rangers with animal companions (of more "normal" sorts than the Echidnox have)
  • One order of monks as well that practice an art focused on tumbling and kicking
    • Myl os Val came from this tribe (Calanon left the Harion and met her here)
    • So Ed's art looks very familiar!
  • Travel with very large beasts—gray renders that have bonded to chosen warriors—used as both beasts of burden and during fighting

Events:

  • Suspicious of massive horde activity, the T'Sen watched them, and learned of the Terminas attack
    • They knew Terminas was infested with demons... but they could not bring themselves to help their ancient enemy.
  • Quite preoccupied with the Terminas situation—who are these "Platypods" and what do they want?
  • Sidolphus, plenipotentiary of Terminas, is in captivity of the T'Sen
    • She fled Terminas when Graz'zt emerged, to seek help, but the T'Sen blamed her for the incident
    • They will only help undo the harm she has done once she admits that the Delphian way of life is irresponsible
  • Sibyl, plenipotentiary of Oraculum, is here as well
    • Had a prophecy about Sidolphus, and the PCs, near Terminas
    • "The beginnings of healing the bond between our peoples"
    • She escaped Oraculum during the confusion of Graz'zt's death
    • She tells the party about the events of Oraculum, the nexus in balance, etc.
    • But the T'Sen treat her similarly, in that they blame her for the state of Delphia, especially since she claims to be a prophet

Problems:

  • Cursed temple gives Asmodeus the erosion power
    • But the Ordered nexuses have magnified its power, so it is now very noticeable
    • Party experiences the past eroding—"the whole lens has changed"
      • Simone does not see the Vanquisher at all—in no reality was he there
      • Simone & Za'har must make Will saves to even remember him being there last session
    • Afterward, Simone can perceive that the probabilities have actually suddenly changed
  • Platypods now believe that Delphia's technology is bringing about the Calamity (due to the demons), and have decided to exterminate the Delphian way of life
    • They probably can be reasoned with, but they are highly dangerous
    • They cannot refresh except in the Wilds—but some of their geomancers stored a large quantity of essence into each of them
    • They distill their own poison and reapply it to their claws—primary Fort save or 4d6 Con, secondary 2d6 Con (DC 20 + 1/2 HD + Con modifier)
    • Claws also destroy metal as a rust monster (Reflex save for attended items is DC 20 + 1/2 HD + Con modifier)
