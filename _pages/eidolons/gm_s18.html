Dragontamer

Why can't Dragontamer be affected by magic? Can he be quaked?

  • He was cursed by the Wendigo, as it died from his killing blow
  • He can be quaked though...

Dragontamer is worried that he got quaked—wants to go home. Worried that Delia doesn't know where he is.

Once they get back, she tells him that while he was away, the curse was broken. They must act quickly.

If they kill Glandur, Delia believes the Orb would transfer to her (the next most powerful white dragon).

Liberating the Orb:

  • Dragontamer willing to pledge troops to retake Oraculum, after some convincing (will make Beardthicket obsolete)

  • "if we take the orb and transfer it to Delia, the fight will be over; the high priests will no longer pose a threat"
  • plan out how to approach Glandur... he lives in an ice cave carved from the frozen lake, a couple miles from the Hell portal
  • they are feasting at his table—but the party arrives during an argument
    • Narylfiel wants the orb transferred to her
    • Fire breath when she doesn't get her way
    • Mordollwen backs Glandur initially, but might turn against him
  • fight with Glandur, other high priests come to his aid
  • give dragon orb to Delia, Dragontamer's sorceress
  • stats for great wyrm dragons...

 

Ironheart

Ironheart has ironwood, but it's in Trimsaran, in the ordered area.

Vartarra has full ironwood body armor (but his helmet gets lost or destroyed during the scuffle).

Battle with Jonas:

  • bring ironwood to Vartarra, PCs "will be notified when it's ready"
  • next morning craft is gone, eladrin captain & gnome engineer tell the party
  • Lineship lies halfway across the barrier, in ruins (extreme weather: hail, lightning, hurricane winds)
  • Jonas vs. Vartarra (w/ Delphian troops) are duking it out in Tat'enda's dead zone
  • Delphians are mostly dead from lightning, Vartarra has protected himself from energy damage
  • Jonas reveals the Guild's assistance in his plot, Vartarra is angry and scared
  • Jonas is a green dragon; when Asmodeus got the 2nd orb (~500 years ago when the Empire was founded), that's when Jonas went over the edge
  • Jonas always rolls a 20 unless opponent is protected by ironwood somehow
  • Jonas's enemies always roll a 1 against him unless they are using ironwood offensively somehow
  • Jonas gets extra actions because he can be in multiple places at the same time
    • as a standard action, Jonas can manifest a "mirror image" effect (1d4+6, maximum 8)
    • the mirror images are dissipated when they take any damage
    • the mirror images, however, each get a turn (unless they came into being during that turn)
