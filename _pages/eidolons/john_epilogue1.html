 Ed's epilogue!

With your permission, I will be making the following merit point changes:

Enemies: Theta (1) Zanzibarianaster  and Moe(3), and (in lack of a better name) the collectors of antiquity (3) (total +7 merit points)

Allies: Alpha (however much you wish to assign), Myl os Val (however much you wish to assign)) (total -2 merit points)

Skill Savant (Knowledge Martial) (whatever is left over)

Grand Total: +/-0 merit points leftover

 

As requested, here's something to work with with respect to the new NPC involvement in Ed's story.  I'll start with the enemies.

 

Theta, as the name would suggest, was not Beta's first replacement.  It was however, the first replacement to survive the initial encounter with its sparing partner.  It did what no other replacement had done, and Alpha was left defeated for the first time since Beta's departure.  Zanzibarianaster and Moe were back in business, but they weren't happy.  With Alpha having gone through so many spare, much time had been wasted, and they were behind schedule.  Instead of rewarding their latest child with praise, they admonished it with criticisms, and stating that "Everything would have been fine if Beta hadn't run off." 

 

From then on Theta strived to please its 'parents' with its efforts, with only reminders of it's inferiority to a warforged it had never met. With every new battle it strove ever harder for praise, but criticism and abuse was its only reward.  And so child like home turned to shame then to bitterness and then to hate. 

 

Theta's involvement in the clash at the big Bridge was entirely a thing of greed.  Zanzibarianaster and Moe needed money to fuel their quest for "the most powerful warforged ever built!" and so they offered their services to whomever would pay.  Although the Delphians who were attacking the fort during the battle weren't of the same city state as the ones Zanzibarianaster  and Moe are allied to, their gold was just as shiny.  The battle was the first time the two had met, and the first time Theta had been shut down by anyone other then Alpha.

 

Zanzibarianaster and Moe are two very motivated gnomes.  Their quest for the "Best Warforged Ever Made!" has been the sole use of their time and resources since before even Alpha was created and Ed was...well... whatever they did to activate him.  Ed's envolvement in the map mission was only sanctioned by the two because it promised to give them a considerable amount of gold, and now that he's gone rogue, with no sign of coming back, they're ticked.  Rebuilding a replacement has cost them dear, and now that they know that Theta was spotted by Ed at the clash of the big bridge, he's a liability.  They want their property back, and they don't particularly care how they arrange it.

 

The Collectors of Antiquity, despite their name, is a criminal syndicate with members in both the technoprophets and the empire.  Originally formed to acquire rare and exotic magic items for the purposes of study, in the hopes of promoting greater understanding, it has long since become an organization  concerned with the acquisition of power through the acquisition of powerful magic items.  After the clash at the big bridge, tales of a warforged who fought off a demon horde and their flaming overseer reached the ears of the Collectors. Their interest piqued, they quickly decided to bring the warforged in, preferably intact, for study, with intent to replicate the design.  With an army of such warforged they could tilt the war in whatever direction they chose, either bringing it to a swift conclusion, or (more likely) to keep the war going indefinately, and make an enormous profit. 

 

Their initial attempts to acquire the designs began with Zanzibarianaster and Moe, but even a cursory examination of their designs showed that there is no possible way they could come up with something so elegant or subtle, and negotiations to purchase the designs ended abruptly (adding further to Zanzibarianaster and Moe''s irritation with their errant property.)  So now they hire bounty-hunters and sell-swords with the intent to bring their new acquisition to the fold.

 

Alpha is still nominally loyal to his 'parents' but has a bond with Beta that they have been unable to purge (not that they're aware it exists)  To date Alpha remains unaware of Zanzibarianaster and Moe's extracurricular activities, and so his initial stance to Beta should they make contact, will be that of the reuniting of brothers.

 

Myl os Val's ears hear many things.  She is now more connected and involved then when she left her student a year or so ago, with an information network that spans the known world.  Content at first to observe, to understand the weave of the war unfolding, the clash at the big bridge, and resulting formation of the Knights of the Common Shield has inspired her to take a more active role in unfolding events.  Although she is unwilling to commit to any one faction, she feels the need to help guide her pupil.   Her stance at current is to help illuminate the path ahead, more likely to provide hints and riddles that lead to answers rather then simply giving the solution.  Her agenda is at present unclear.
