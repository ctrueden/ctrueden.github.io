Io

  • View
  • Edit
  • Outline
  • Grant

Io, the two-faced dragon god, consists of two parts: Bahamut and Tiamat. Though he is very powerful (rank 15), which parts of Io are dominant depend on who controls the two Orbs of Dragonkind, unique artifacts tied to its psyche. (These orbs cannot be copied by flow surges, just as humans can't.)

Bahamut is the lawful good aspect of Io; Tiamat is the chaotic evil aspect. Io itself is a two-faced god of balance and neutrality, believing in conflict as a means of preserving and enriching the world.

Currently, Asmodeus controls both Orbs, so Tiamat is dominant, and Bahamut suppressed. Under "normal" circumstances, the high priest of Bahamut would control one Orb, with the high priest of Tiamat controlling the other.

Dardanos Peacekeeper, though he was the high priest of Bahamut for centuries, never controlled an Orb. However, the first Emperor (who later became Baalzebul) did possess one.
