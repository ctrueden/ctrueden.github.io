Simone's Journal

Submitted by andy on Sat, 2010-06-26 07:50.

Story time! Everyone that I'm traveling with is special in some way. They are all quite powerful and have lived such interesting lives. Almost everyone is missing a piece or two, and Sahrit even seems to have two pasts. I wonder if he has two presents as well.



I was a little nervous about exposing their histories, lest some secret get loose that they would prefer to keep. I'm sure I got close to that line, but I don't think I crossed it. A few people were unaware of the specifics of some items of their past, such as Biger's crank getting attached, or Eidylol's long history.

Joseph seemed relieved to have his secret exposed. Or maybe it was that we didn't persecute him for his gift, and that we heard him out. He introduced us to Ann, the remains of the little girl he withered to death. She seems like a sweet and playful little girl, though Joseph claimed I was the first person she had warmed up to. Warms the heart. I hope he can do right by her. And I hope I can chat with her some more at some point soon.

Thankfully Joseph's confession took ran until another major quake happened and we didn't get to Kith. Kith has an ugly past, and some ugly secrets that I'm sure he'd prefer to remain secret. I prefer they remain secret as well.

I told him that I knew that he killed the emperor. He thought that was a bold accusation. I think he misunderstood me, I wasn't looking for confirmation. I explained that I saw it in his past, and that the scroll that I showed him was for the one who assassinated the emperor. I explained that I had no problems with his actions, and I wanted to help him keep his secret. He replied that he was glad that he didn't have to kill me, and acknowledged that either could kill the other if we so chose.

So, as I said, as Joseph finished, another large quake happened. Biger and Sahrit vanished and were replaced with Haroun and Arwel. Arwel is a Dragonborn that I've met previously, as she runs a small hostel. She's quite the accomplished fighter, though she likes to go on about her training a bit much.

Arwel also had the Emperor-slayer sword. He was part of a group that was hired to transport the sword, but they were under attack when the quake happened. Not sure how it got to them (or if it's even the exact same sword). But the sword

Haroun is new to me. He's a sickly Genasi Geomancer with a mix of Earth and Water. I'm not sure what he story is. He's lived a sheltered life, and seems to have sacrificed his heath to be a prophet. Arwel is acting as his guide and has been working to educate him about the world. I would love to get a chance to learn more, but he'll have to warm up to me first.

As we traveled, we managed to find a clue in the attack. We found the spot where a single orc expanded over and over again to form the army. I could tell that the effect came from the nearby techno-prophet city.

We were headed near that direction anyways, so we continued. Haroun proved to be a liability here though. He didn't want to go near the city, but it's not safe to leave people in the wilderness, and there really is no other place to go. Joseph tried to disguise him, but the rest of us provided our own little touches. He looked like a straight water genasi by the time we were done.

Our trip to the city provided a fun diversion. I wish I could have stayed longer. We stopped by a tavern by the entrance. Thankfully it was early, and not many people were around. I bought a few drinks for Haroun and Arwel while the rest of us left to take care of business.

Ed went to the library, while I took Kith and Joseph to the nexus. I studied the nexus for a few minutes to see that the affected orc did pass through the nexus, it didn't originate here. I was approached by a local geomancer mentioned that someone teleported into the city through the nexus on an errand around that time, and it was possible that the orc used that to piggyback passage. Not much is known about the person or the errand though.

An effect like this could only come from a very powerful geomancer, or group of geomancers. Seems like the source was further into the woods, but we couldn't tell more than that. Also, we had no idea who could have been involved in something like this. We discussed the idea that the current quakes are the result of the magics used in this assault, and given the power required, might be on the mark. But this kind of power is unprecedented, at least in my experience.

?? (not sure exactly how this played out (or if it matters))

We returned to the geomancer at the nexus with Arwel's sword. She explains that this was supposed to be it's ultimate destination, and they wanted to study it. (I wonder if they have caught wind of Iraja's prophesy?).

Haroun saw that someone was after the sword in the city. The geomancer graced us with a few enchantments (pass without trace) and we were out the door. We're to take the sword to Terminus. Yay... Since we need to stay hidden, it will take almost a year to get there.

Not long after, Haroun received a message that our destination has been compromised, and we are to just stay hidden and keep the sword safe. We change our plans and start heading towards an independent city bu the swamp. I haven't explored the swamp, so I am looking forward to this.

A Fey Tale.

Submitted by paul on Fri, 2009-09-25 21:05.

Kithlandil the guide.

There once was a wee tiny person named Kithlandil. He was part man, and part bug!

Well, Kithlandil was leading some very strange people through a very dangerous place.

See the Tall Tall Tall Halfling! He speaks to the runes and to his ghostly daughter.

See the Metal Warrior! It smashes and pounds, and sees many places.

See the Sickly Prophet! He bumbles and cringes, and remembers the future.

See the Devout Scaley Woman! She holds the ancient sword, and wards the prophet.

See the Pretty Lady! She is Kithlandil's bestest friend ever!

They walked, and they walked, and they walked some more, planning and plotting to protect the sword.

Where would they take it?

To the City! said the sword's warden. We'll keep it safe there!

To the Feywild! said the Pretty Lady. It will be a grand adventure!

To the center of the earth! said dour and stodgy Kithlandil. No one will find it there.

No! said the Prophet. It will surely be lost in the center of the earth!

No! said the Halfling. The Feywild is dangerous!

So the stout party went to the City, as the warden had advised.

But the sword was not safe in the City, for the city could not protect it.

Go! Fly far away! Take it across the land, far from this place!

So the adventurers left the City, to cross the land.

Woe, woe, woe to the guardians of the sword! For before you and behind you it is lost!

To the Swamp! said the Warrior. I have not been there, and it is a place of great power.

To the swamp they went, each keeping their secrets in their hearts.

But Kithlandil was watching...

Further Journaling from Necro Joe

Submitted by mark on Sat, 2009-08-22 14:45.

Journal of Joesph Vernor

(you again skip ahead to the next entry)

Entry 9: Confessions

Another day of firsts.  I think this is the first time I've ever talked about that night before.  To anyone who didn't then start throwing my stuff around with telekinesis.  Simone somehow knew about our pasts, maybe some kind of Geomantic power?  I just started gushing about it.  They took it very well suprisingly.  I mean, I turn people into withered undead!  At the very least I leave them 'crippled' for the rest of their lives.  All to give a second chance at life to street urchins I find.  Just cause of my guilt about Anne.

Thats the other thing!  Anne played hide and go seek with Simone.  That...I...don't have any frame of reference here.  I've never seen her playful before.  Maybe its because of Simone's charisma, maybe its cause she is a fellow elf?  Maybe I need to lighten up and be more of a companion to her then a 'master'.  How do I get her out of her shell more?  I think I should try and involve her more with the group, so that she has an opportunity to interact with others more.  But I think I'm also going to try talking with her more.  Maybe in meditation?  We'll see.

I also learned some interesting facts about my companions, namely that Sahrit knows that Necromancer who killed me.  He called it something strange...Lich.  There is something oddly fitting about calling it that.  He also called it by name, Vance Doubain, I must do as much research as possible to learn more about this figure.  I have a feeling I will meet him again, and this time he will kill me more permanently.  Maybe this name will lead me to those other two figures?

Then, suddenly, another quake happened.  Another BIG one.  And just like that Sahrit and Biger were gone, and in their place were a large Dragonborn and a sickly looking Eladrin.  The Dragonborn is female if my placing of crest coloration is correct, but its always hard to tell with the lizards.  Coloration is odd however, what kind of breathweapon is possesses is totally up in the air.  The Eladrin seems to be have traits of both Earth and Water elementals, another odd figure.

More interestingly though, was the weapon the Dragonborn(Arwell) was carrying.  It seems it was the same sword used to kill the emperor.  Possibly its what caused all the Dragonborn to become ill during the assault.  How it ended up in her possession is unknown however.  Those necromancers were also apparently intersted in it.  I should probably study the sword myself, so I might better understand my foes desire.

The Eladrin(Haroun) is very odd.  I would almost place him as being mentally deficient, but Arwell insists that he is mearly sheltered.  Whatever it is, he seems to be some form of prophet?

More interestingly, it seems they know Isaline.  In fact they were traveling with her when the shift happened.  Could we be part of some kind of link between others traveling elsewhere?  I should try to talk with Arwell about this, so that we can know what to expect if we get shifted as well.  If we are to expect further shifts like this, we should be prepared to work with them.

We decided to continue our tracking of the orcs, and went on our way, hopefully we will be able to get some answers.  I have a bad feeling that sword will lead further trouble our way though.

 

Entry 10: Growth

Its hard to pinpoint the actual reason, but I think it might have something to do with my close brush with death at Eloyes.  I have made leaps and bounds with my magical study, all of my runes are much crisper and less wasteful now that I have been practicing like this every night.  I think I've even found a way to increase my ability to store undead essence, but its not easy to do.  I shall have to work on that process.  I was able to extend the distance I can travel with my Shadow Jump however.  I'm sure that will come in handy.

Anne has remained quiet after her little jaunt with Simone, but I expect she is shy now that we have two newcomers.

Arwell allowed me to study the blade however.  Very interestingly, it seems related to my withered necromancy.  I felt I might be able to wither/unwither through the sword, as opposed to my normal limit of physical touch.  However, I couldn't help but notice the similarity between this sword and the kind of power that left that mark on Sahrit's shoulder.  Whoever made this sword was unbelievably powerful.  And for what purpose, I have no idea.  It certainly wasn't for the cheap parlor tricks I do.  I fear its true purpose is some kind of genocide.

 

Entry 11: Origin

We found the orc origin point finally, and as we suspected they appeared out of nowhere.  Some kind of teleportation effect it seems?  Maybe thats how those weird orcs happened, they got lost somehow during transit through the nexus/leylines and became horribly corrupted with its energies.  But my expertese is decidedly not within the realms of Geomancy, so I could be totally wrong there.

Simone mentioned that they seemd to somehow...duplicated from a single orc who appeared in the woods here.  Once they finished, they built a camp and seige weapons and made their way towards Eloyes.

So powerful was whatever effect used on the orcs to do this, that even now it was still lingering.  Chilling that we seem to be putting ourselves in such a foes path.

Simone was able to discern the direction of the origin point of the orc/s teleportation, and we have set off in that direction.  Hopefully the closest Technoprophet city will have more answers.

 

Entry 12: Kill it with Fire

Sometimes I wonder how I got wrapped up in these events, other then my damnable curiousity.  I suppose thats what drove me to explore my magic, but still, I seem to be looking at eventual confrontations with an unbelievably powerful cabal of Necromancers and possibly a powerful cabal of Geomancers.  Or just an increadibly strong one.

We got many of the answers we were seeking in the Technoprophet city of [CITY].  It seems Haroun was very distraught at traveling within one, but because of my spectacular disguising skills with a little bit of Simone's help, we were able to hide his identity.  Kinda wish she'd used the billowing cloak though.

Anyways, I traveled with Simone to the Nexus to ask about the orcs.  On the way I suddenly had a cat in my haversack.  I...really hope I'm not going crazy and that it is Kith's doing.  I don't need any more phobias about random cats.

We found out that apparently they had a breach of sorts and that the orc got teleported without their knowledge, which doesn't impress me about the security.  But then we are talking about foes that are strong enough to somehow compress an army of orcs into one body...but I'm getting ahead of myself.

We were eventually called away by Ed, who had found a gnome, who seemed to be fairly knowledgable about these matters.  I tried asking him about what he might know about the necromancers, but...sadly he just kept suggesting to burn things.  Considering I am a necromancer, I felt a tad bit uncomfortable.  Sadly I think I may have encouraged him to build some kind of Weapon of Mass Destruction centered on Fire.

(some sketched runes that seem to be focused on ways of defending oneself from fire)

However he was quite a bit more knowledgeable about the events surrounding the sword and the orcs.  In fact it seemed this was the city that was to have recieved the sword, but the line ship that was carrying it disappeared.

Ed went off to inform Arwel, who joined us shortly.  The gnome then took us to the people who were to recieve the sword, which turned out to be the woman we were speaking with earlier.  Arwel produced the sword, and put the woman into a state of shock it seemed.

Apparently they were to have studied the blade there, but after the attacks on the Line ship and Eloyes, we did not feel the city would be safe with the sword present.  She agreed and told us to take the city on a more then 12 month journey to Terminus!  And thats if we were to cut directly through the Wastes and the Empire.

We think that perhaps the orc attack was meant as a way to get the empire to go to full war with the Technoprophets, perhaps to mask their actions somewhat.  Whatever they are.

About a day out of the Technoprophet city, it seems our fears and Haroun's prophecy were correct, as the woman gain contacted Arwel, to inform her that our destination was known, and that we need to simply keep the sword out of our enemies hands.  Well, at least our journey will be shorter.

We are going to try to go to the closest independant city, and figure out where to go from there.  Maybe the oracle mentioned by the gnome could possibly help, though he seemed pretty adament about how we shouldn't go there.  About as adament as he was about setting fire to necromancers.

I'm going to spend tonight trying to teach Anne her basic runes.  I'm trying to give her some kind of basic education, to see if any of the knowledge will stay with her.  If it does, it means it might be possible to help her mature, to prevent her destruction from her permanent innocence.
