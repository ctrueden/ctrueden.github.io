Gathering Forces

Submitted by jesse on Fri, 2010-05-28 15:43.

Well our search for military forces led us to Elyria and an encounter with Marquis Ironheart.  After some brief negotiations he agreed to assist us so long as we put a stop to Jonas and the ordering.  Sometimes it just that simple eh?

Ironheart also informed us of the current plan of the Delphians in Arx to construct an ironwood lineship to breach Tat'enda.  However the Delphians would need the Imperial stockpile of ironwood in order to build such a vessel.  In an unsurprising change of pace, or a continuation of bad luck, the stockpile is in the ordered city of Trimsaran.  While I do not understand the magical powers of ironwood, I see the importance of retaking that cache.  Jonas might be insane to do these things, but he is no fool to cut us off from the stockpile.  With this information several of us took off for 'holiday' in Arx.

Lo and behold, the Delphians were already along on their plan upon our arrival but one suit of armor was not enough.  When we informed Silius, who is in charge of the construction, of the Imperial stockpile a mission to raid Trimsaran was quickly ordered.  A little to my surprise the mission went off without a hitch.  The soldier reported the city to be completely empty.  It seems a little foolish that with an infinite number of soldiers no guards were assigned to this critical weakness.  The small ship was completed quickly enough after, but unsurprisingly misfortune struck.  The low and treacherous Silius dominated the crew and took the ship in the night.

We quickly tracked them down but it seems that Jonas, or rather Calanon, had made short work of them.  Why does the fact that an elder green wyrm would lead a nomad tribe not surprise me any longer?  I chose to strike at the greater foe but Calanon's geomancy was beyond even my power.  Silius fled like the scum he is through the ordering and I am unsure what has happened to him.  Once equipped with ironwood Vashir and were able destroy several of Calanon's images, but Biger and Dariel saved us from being overwhelmed.  Still he was likely the greatest threat I have yet faced.  He accepted an offer to have a mark of justice placed on him to prevent further attacks.  While Ironheart was brought in to oversee the agreement that we had made a drastic change occurred in the beast.  It seems that one of the Orbs of Dragonkind is now possessed by another.  I can only imagine it was the work of the others, but the exact details I can hardly imagine.

So now we seem to be set to have the war council and begin the assault on Oraculum.  I feel unsure what must be done beyond retaking the dual nexuses.  The things that once mattered most to me are now uncertain: returning to the Empire is not as appealing as it once was, I cannot simply live with my family knowing what I now know, I do not trust the gods to any significant degree, learning the names of my parents and how they came to be human gives me little solace after forty two years, and the fact that as a human I possess a single unknown destiny (unlike almost all the others) is no comfort.  All that seems to be left is to fight and some day die against those who would use this world to their own desires be they a demon, devil, god, or mortals.  If that is what must be then I shall become such a force that those who survive shall never forget my name.
